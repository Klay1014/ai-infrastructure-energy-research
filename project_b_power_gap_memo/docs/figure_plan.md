Line chart or stacked driver schematic.

## Figure 2: Taiwan Electricity System Snapshot

Question:

What does Taiwan's electricity system look like today?

Chart type:

Stacked bar for generation mix plus side table for annual generation, peak load, night peak, reserve margin.

Data:

- MOEA/Taipower generation and peak-load data
- Energy Information Platform generation mix

Takeaway:

Taiwan's adequacy question must separate annual energy from peak and night-peak adequacy.

## Figure 3: Power Adequacy Funnel

Question:

Where can a power gap appear?

Diagram:

```text
Annual demand
-> peak load
-> night peak
-> dispatchable capacity
-> operating reserve
-> clean-power availability
-> grid/fuel resilience
```

Takeaway:

"Enough electricity" is not one number.

## Figure 4: Taiwan Gap Scenario Matrix

Question:

Under what scenarios does Taiwan become tight?

Chart type:

Scenario matrix.

Axes:

- Demand growth: official baseline vs high AI/semiconductor load
- Supply delivery: on schedule vs delayed

Takeaway:

Taiwan's risk is most severe when AI/fab load grows faster than planned and new capacity or grid upgrades slip.
