# Memo Outline

Title: Is Taiwan's Power System Ready For AI Infrastructure Growth?

Subtitle: A Public-Signal Comparison Of Global Electricity Demand, Taiwan Power Adequacy, And Semiconductor Load Pressure

## 1. Executive Summary

Question:

Is Taiwan's electricity system adequate for AI and semiconductor growth, and what type of gap matters most?

Core message draft:

Global electricity demand is accelerating as AI, data centers, advanced manufacturing, EVs, air conditioning, and electrification increase load. Taiwan is exposed because it is a core semiconductor manufacturing hub. However, the right question is not simply annual electricity sufficiency. Taiwan's power risk must be decomposed into annual energy, peak load, night peak, operating reserve, planning reserve, grid constraints, fuel import resilience, and clean-power availability.

## 2. Global Electricity Demand Context

Questions:

- How fast is global electricity demand growing?
- How much of the growth is linked to AI/data centers and advanced manufacturing?
- Is the global issue annual energy, local grid capacity, or peak load?

Signals to collect:

- IEA global electricity demand growth forecast
- Data center electricity demand estimates
- Regional stress examples: US, Europe, Asia
- Industrial electrification and cooling demand

## 3. Taiwan Electricity Profile

Questions:

- How much electricity does Taiwan use and generate annually?
- What is Taiwan's generation mix?
- How much does Taiwan rely on imported fuels?
- What changed after nuclear phase-out?

Signals to collect:

- Annual generation and consumption
- Installed capacity and generation mix
- LNG/coal share
- Renewable share
- Nuclear share after 2025

## 4. Demand Drivers In Taiwan

Questions:

- How much of future load comes from semiconductors, AI data centers, electrification, cooling, and general economic growth?
- Is demand concentrated in science parks or geographically dispersed?

Signals to collect:

- MOEA/Taipower demand forecast
- Science park load
- Semiconductor fab electricity intensity
- AI data center project announcements
- Industrial electricity sales

## 5. Supply Adequacy Framework

Questions:

- Is Taiwan sufficient on annual energy?
- Is Taiwan sufficient on peak capacity?
- Is night peak the binding constraint?
- What reserve margin is considered adequate?

Definitions:

- Annual energy: TWh/year
- Peak load: GW at maximum demand moment
- Operating reserve margin: daily dispatchable buffer
- Planning reserve margin: long-term capacity adequacy target
- Night peak: evening peak when solar generation falls

## 6. Energy Gap Scenario Model

The memo should present scenarios, not a single fake-precise forecast.

Simple structure:

```text
Projected gap =
projected peak load
- firm available capacity
- reserve requirement
```

Annual structure:

```text
annual gap =
projected annual electricity demand
- projected annual generation / imports / demand response
```

Scenario inputs:

- Base demand growth
- AI/data center incremental load
- Semiconductor incremental load
- New gas, renewable, storage, and demand-response capacity
- Night-peak solar contribution
- Required reserve margin

## 7. Risk Matrix

| Risk | Easing Signal | Worsening Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Annual demand growth | Demand grows near official forecast | Demand rises faster from AI/fabs |
| Night peak | Storage, gas, demand response reduce evening pressure | Solar-heavy system creates sharper evening ramp |
| Clean power | Renewables and corporate PPAs scale | Semiconductor customers need more green power than available |
| Fuel resilience | LNG storage and import routes diversified | LNG supply disruption or price spike |
| Grid | Transmission upgrades and science park planning keep pace | New loads locate outside planned capacity zones |

## 8. What To Track Next

- Taipower daily peak and reserve margin
- MOEA power supply-demand report
- Annual electricity sales by sector
- Science park and semiconductor load announcements
- AI data center siting and power contract news
- Renewable generation and storage additions
- LNG terminal capacity and fuel inventory
- Corporate renewable procurement from TSMC and peers

## 9. Limitations

- Public data may not reveal project-level load.
- Official forecasts can change with economic growth, technology deployment, and weather.
- Peak adequacy is more important than annual totals but harder to forecast.
- Clean-power adequacy is different from total-power adequacy.
