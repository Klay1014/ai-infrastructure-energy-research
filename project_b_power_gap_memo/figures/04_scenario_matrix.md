# Figure 4: Taiwan Temperature-Power Scenario Matrix

| Demand / Supply Case | Supply On Schedule | Supply Or Grid Delayed |
|---|---|---|
| Official baseline demand | Manageable if heat stays within recent operating conditions and reserve margin remains adequate | Watch list: official annual balance may hold while local or evening constraints appear |
| High heat + warm nights | Night-peak pressure rises; demand response and storage become more important | High risk: cooling demand persists after solar falls while flexibility is limited |
| High AI / semiconductor load | Structural demand growth; official baseline may need upward revision | Highest risk: load growth and delivery constraints align |
| Lower demand / mild summer | Easing | Mixed: supply delay may be manageable but still weakens resilience |

Takeaway: The most important risk is not a generic annual shortage. It is the alignment of high heat, persistent cooling demand, industrial load growth, and delayed supply or grid delivery.
